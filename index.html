About

From the formations of Grotto to the release of their debut album.

  • Summer 2013

    Basement Recording Sessions

    Grotto began in Dan Taro's basement the summer before he started highschool. Alec Fliescher played bass and guitar and played shows often with Grotto's resident drummer, Nate Ball. The two joined forces with Taro on an electronic spin on their own projects. They trio got together in Taro's basement many times over the summer recording various ideas and working on remix competitons.

  • 2014-2018

    Composition of Spring Drips

    Grotto's debut album came about because Taro manipulated the recordings from the previous summer and wrote full arrangements around the recording sessions of Ball and Fliescher. Taro worked tirelessly every morning to create and compose on his hour long commute to school on the bus. During the year, Fliescher left Denver and the band became a duo: Taro on electronics and controllers and Ball on percussion.

Spring Drips

Grotto's Debut Album

Dan Taro has found his voice on Spring Drips. This is the first full-length release from the Denver-based artist. The album shows the tenacity and focus of Taroâ€™s musical career.

Spring Drips started in the summer of 2014 when Taro decided to create a band to bridge the gap between cold electronic sounds and human warmth. Over the course of that year, Taro spent each morning creating the foundation for the album. Through experimentation, songs began to take shape around bass lines, rhythms, or the timbres of different instruments.

Over sessions with Nate Ball, the resident drummer of the project, the two worked tirelessly recording in three different studios over the course of the next three and a half years. Other long-time friends made appearances in various songs to add another layer to the album.

All this culminated in the massive, sprawling, and complex work that is Spring Drips.
